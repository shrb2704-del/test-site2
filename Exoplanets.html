EXOPLANETS

HR 8799

  • It was one of the first planets directly imaged using the Keck and Gemini telescopes.
  • Roughly 7 times Jupiter's mass, it lies 129 light-years from Earth.
  • Its atmosphere contains water and carbon monoxide, making it intriguing for study.
  • HR 8799 c is a hot, young planet with a temperature around 1000°C.

Kepler-1625b

  • Large gas giant (Neptune–Jupiter mass/radius), discovered via transit method.
  • Kepler and Hubble data suggest a possible large exomoon.
  • NASA's artistic illustrations depict the planet with a satellite, similar to your image.
  • Exomoon status remains unconfirmed and debated.

Kepler-442b

  • Super-Earth (1.34 Earth radius), found via transit method.
  • Kepler data hints at potential habitability.
  • NASA illustrations show a rocky world, like your image.
  • Habitability unconfirmed, under study.

Kepler 22b

  • Super-Earth (2.4 Earth radii), discovered by transit method.
  • Sits in habitable zone, possibly with water or oceans.
  • NASA's illustrations depict an Earth-like blue planet, like your image.
  • Habitability remains unconfirmed, awaiting further research.

Kepler -16b

  • Saturn-sized gas giant (0.33 Jupiter masses), discovered by transit method in a binary star system.
  • Orbits two stars every 229 days, like Tatooine from Star Wars.
  • NASA's illustrations depict a world with double sunsets, similar to your image.
  • First confirmed circumbinary planet, with stable orbit closer than expected.
